About the Project

Who We Are & What We're Doing

We are a Master of Urban Planning studio group at the University of Washington, working this quarter alongside Liveable City Year @ UW and Pierce County on the question of incorporation for South Hill, WA.

South Hill is one of the largest unincorporated communities in Washington State — with a population of over 70,000 residents — yet it has no locally elected city government. Pierce County currently provides most local services.

Our role this quarter is focused on community engagement: making sure residents have access to clear information and a genuine opportunity to weigh in. We are not here to advocate for a particular outcome — we are here to inform, listen, and create space for community conversation.

01

Community Engagement Approaches

Identifying meaningful, accessible ways to reach South Hill residents and hear from a broad cross-section of the community.

02

Informational Materials

Developing plain-language resources — like this website and our Incorporation Primer — to help residents understand the question and engage in an informed discussion.

03

Foundational Discussions

Facilitating community conversations — including our Open House — to surface concerns, opportunities, and priorities directly from the people who live and work in South Hill.

04

Building Community Involvement

Supporting ongoing community awareness and participation so residents remain engaged and informed as this conversation continues beyond our studio quarter.

The Basics

What Is Incorporation?

Incorporation is the process by which an unincorporated community formally becomes a city or town. Here's what that means in plain language.

🏛️

Local Government

An incorporated city has its own elected city council and mayor who make decisions about local services, budgets, land use, and policy — rather than relying on county government to manage these functions.

💰

Revenue & Taxes

Cities collect their own taxes (like sales tax) and fees. This revenue stays local and can be directed toward community priorities like roads, parks, and public safety.

🚔

Services & Infrastructure

An incorporated city can operate its own police department, manage its own roads, set zoning rules, and control development — giving residents more direct say over their neighborhood's character.

🗳️

The Process

In Washington State, incorporation requires a feasibility study, a boundary review by the Boundary Review Board, and ultimately a public vote. The community itself decides whether to incorporate.

⚖️

Tradeoffs

Incorporation brings more local control but also more local responsibility. Costs and services shift from the county to the new city. Understanding this balance is central to our research.

📍

South Hill Today

Currently, South Hill is governed as an unincorporated area of Pierce County. Residents pay county taxes and receive county services, but have no locally elected city representatives.

Our Scope This Quarter

What We're Exploring — and What We're Not

As a student studio group with one quarter, our work is focused on community engagement — not technical analysis. Here's how we fit into the bigger picture:

A

Our Role: Community Engagement

Our primary focus is people, not spreadsheets. We are developing informational materials, hosting community events, and creating space for residents to learn about the incorporation question and share their perspectives. This website is part of that work.

B

Fiscal Analysis — Led by Pierce County

The in-depth fiscal feasibility analysis — examining revenues, costs, and the financial picture of potential incorporation — is being conducted by an independent consulting firm contracted by Pierce County. We will share their findings with the community as they become available.

C

Services & Boundaries — Starting the Conversation

We are helping residents think through foundational questions: What services do you rely on today? What might change? What area should be included in any potential city? These are conversation-starters, not conclusions — the full analysis goes well beyond our scope.

D

Governance — Raising the Questions

What might a local government for South Hill look like? We may surface this question in community discussions, but a thorough governance analysis is beyond what our studio is tasked with this quarter. For now, we want to know what residents think matters most.

E

What You Think — The Most Important Input

Ultimately, any decision about South Hill's future belongs to the people who live there. Our job is to make sure you have accurate, accessible information and a real chance to weigh in. That's what this project is about.
