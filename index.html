About the Project

Who We Are & What We're Doing

We are a studio group from the University of Washington working alongside Liveable City Year @ UW and Pierce County to study the feasibility and community readiness for South Hill, WA to incorporate as its own city.

South Hill is one of the largest unincorporated communities in Washington State — with a population of over 70,000 residents — yet it currently operates without the full suite of local government services that incorporated cities provide.

Our goal is not to advocate for a particular outcome, but to inform and listen. We want residents to have access to clear, accurate information and a platform to make their voices heard.

01

Research

We are gathering data on South Hill's demographics, fiscal landscape, infrastructure, and governance to build a clear picture of current conditions.

02

Outreach

We are conducting surveys, community meetings, and conversations with local stakeholders to understand what residents value and want for their community's future.

03

Analysis

We are examining what incorporation could look like structurally, fiscally, and politically — and sharing that information back with the community.

The Basics

What Is Incorporation?

Incorporation is the process by which an unincorporated community formally becomes a city or town. Here's what that means in plain language.

🏛️

Local Government

An incorporated city has its own elected city council and mayor who make decisions about local services, budgets, land use, and policy — rather than relying on county government to manage these functions.

💰

Revenue & Taxes

Cities collect their own taxes (like sales tax) and fees. This revenue stays local and can be directed toward community priorities like roads, parks, and public safety.

🚔

Services & Infrastructure

An incorporated city can operate its own police department, manage its own roads, set zoning rules, and control development — giving residents more direct say over their neighborhood's character.

🗳️

The Process

In Washington State, incorporation requires a feasibility study, a boundary review by the Boundary Review Board, and ultimately a public vote. The community itself decides whether to incorporate.

⚖️

Tradeoffs

Incorporation brings more local control but also more local responsibility. Costs and services shift from the county to the new city. Understanding this balance is central to our research.

📍

South Hill Today

Currently, South Hill is governed as an unincorporated area of Pierce County. Residents pay county taxes and receive county services, but have no locally elected city representatives.

Looking Ahead

What Could Incorporation Look Like for South Hill?

Our research explores a range of scenarios. Here are some of the key questions we are working to answer:

A

Fiscal Feasibility

Could South Hill sustain itself financially as a city? We are analyzing potential tax revenues, existing service costs, and transition expenses to model what incorporation could look like on a balance sheet.

B

Service Continuity

How would services like roads, parks, and public safety be managed during and after a transition? We are looking at how similar communities navigated this process.

C

Boundary Options

What area would the city encompass? Boundary decisions affect who is included, what tax base is available, and how services are delivered. We are mapping and analyzing different potential boundaries.

D

Governance Structure

What kind of city government would best serve South Hill? Strong mayor? Council-manager? We are examining models that have worked in communities of similar size and character.

E

Community Identity

What do residents want South Hill to feel like in 20 years? Local control can preserve community character — but only if residents shape the vision. This is why your voice matters.
